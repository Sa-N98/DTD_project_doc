Design Thinking for

A project-based course where you identify a real user problem, apply the KDT-EAST design thinking framework, and build a working app prototype — from empathize to launch.
Credits: 4 (BS) / 12 (BTech)
Team size: 4 members
Duration: Weeks 1–10
Project weight: 40% of final grade

Course overview

This course equips you with the skills and mindset of a design thinker, enabling you to create impactful, user-centered solutions to real-world problems. Through a series of milestones, you'll apply the KDT-EAST framework to identify a problem, empathize with users, ideate solutions, prototype an app, and test it with real users.


By the end of the course, you'll have a working prototype that addresses a genuine user need, along with experience navigating the full design thinking process.

Step 1
Identify
Find a real user problem through interviews, personas, and customer journey maps.
Step 2
Design
Apply KDT-EAST — analyze root causes, brainstorm ideas, and wireframe your solution.
Step 3
Build & Launch
Develop a working app prototype, test with users, and pitch your product.

Important rules

Milestone deadlines will not be extended. Plan ahead — late submissions are not accepted.
Teams not formed on time will be allocated by the course team with a 1-mark penalty.
Existing solutions identified in GP0 cannot be cloned as your GP3 solution.
Team members cannot be counted as users or customers in any interview or test phase.
