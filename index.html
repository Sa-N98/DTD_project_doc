Milestone 2

Timeline: Week 4 to Week 6

Submission Date: November 10, 2024

Marks: 25

Evaluation & Score Release: Before Week 8

1. Analyze and Solve Phase

Add Visitor link to Mural

Canvas 2

  • Pick n problems and analyze using the Multi-why technique to explore root causes. (3 marks – Individual)
  • Create n conflicts of interest and define desired results. (3 marks – Individual)
  • Generate n How Might We (HMW) questions. (2 marks – Individual)

Canvas 3

  • Generate a variety of ideas using silent brainstorming. (3 marks – Individual + Team)
  • Develop wireframes of the prototype using Figma/any toolkit (Note: A functional app is NOT required at this stage). (5 marks – Team)

n = Number of team members

2. Marketing Features

Create the following app components: (2 marks – Team)

  • App title and developer’s name
  • App icon
  • App features and category
  • Short description (include relevant keywords)

Update Jira (integrated with Mural) to track the project.

3. Bulletproof Your Concept

Provide videos showcasing interaction with a peer (not from your team). Both presenter and peer must keep cameras on and display name & roll number.

Full discussion can be recorded for project use and submission. (6 marks – Team/Dissenters or Individual/Presenter)

Presentation Requirements

  • Presenter must clearly explain:
    • Problem statement
    • Empathize phase findings
    • HMWs formed during Analyze phase
    • Planned next steps for Solve phase
  • One-way presentation (only presenter speaks)
  • Approximate duration: 8 minutes

Ritual Dissent (RD)

Ritual Dissent 1 (Sample)

One member presents Empathize & Analyze phases to another team. Dissenters provide feedback. Presenter listens and records feedback without refuting.

  • 8 minutes presentation
  • 8 minutes feedback
  • Total: 16 minutes
  • Do NOT work on Solve phase yet

Ritual Dissent 2 (Sample)

After reworking based on feedback, another member presents updated work to a new team.

Tip: You are not required to fix everything, but discuss all feedback within your team.

Important: Dissenters/Assenters must keep cameras ON when asking questions.

Deadline: November 10, 2024

Milestone 3

Timeline: Week 7 to Week 10

Submission Dates:

  • PART 1 – December 5, 2024
  • PART 2 – December 9, 2024

Marks: 50 (Milestone 3 + Presentation & Viva)

Evaluation: Before End-term Exam

1. Test Phase Videos

Provide m recorded videos (each 5 minutes) capturing customers testing the final prototype.

Requirements:

  • Camera ON and audio clear
  • Display name and roll number while interacting
  • Each team member conducts at least one interview
  • Test users must be different from Empathize phase
  • Users should represent diverse backgrounds (age, education, work experience, non-IITM BS where possible)

m = Number of team members + 2

Do not guide customers during testing. Observe behaviors, difficulties, ignored features, and document feedback in revised CJMs.

2. Canvas 4

  • Empathize 2 (P2) Canvas with revised n Customer Journey Maps (CJMs)
  • Identification of new challenges/issues introduced by the app
  • Analyze 2 (P2) – Canvas
  • Solve 2 (P2) – Canvas
  • Post-prototype field study iterations & final prototype updates

n = Number of team members

3. Marketing Video

Create a brief and engaging marketing video (30–60 seconds). Must NOT be AI-generated.

4. Project Closure Report (2–3 Pages)

(a) KDT-EAST Phases Followed (1 Page)

(b) Product/Service/App Features & Assessment (1 Page)

  • Human Desirability
  • Business Viability
  • Technological Feasibility

(c) Tools Used & Lessons Learnt (1 Page)

  • Tools and platforms used
  • Accomplishments
  • Lessons learnt
  • Work-in-progress & tentative closure date

Important Report Instructions:

  • Do NOT include links (Figma, Mural, Jira, etc.)
  • Include IIT Madras BS logo
  • Include BS Degree details
  • Include full names of team members & team number only
  • Do NOT include roll numbers or emails

5. Final App Prototype (Mandatory)

Submit the final prototype built using a low-code or no-code platform.

AI plugins may generate features/wireframes — use them thoughtfully.

Internal evaluation will verify whether Solve phase ideas are properly translated into app features.

6. Final Presentation (Recorded)

  • Total duration: 5–7 minutes
  • Include 30–60 sec marketing video
  • Explain KDT process (Desirability, Viability, Feasibility)
  • Wireframes demo / App walkthrough
  • Camera ON, display name (Roll no. not required), audio clear
